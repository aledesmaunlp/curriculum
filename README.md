## 2. Publications | Publicaciones

**Ledesma, Adrian (2022). Ucrania: la punta del iceberg. Hacia un nuevo hegemón y un nuevo sistema de relaciones internacionales**
[Ukraine: The Tip of the Iceberg. Towards a New Hegemon and a New System of International Relations]
Paper presented at the XI Sociology Conference, National University of La Plata (UNLP).

XI Jornadas de Sociología de la Universidad Nacional de La Plata (UNLP), Mesa 9: Dinámica de la crisis global. Hacia una Geopolítica del siglo XXI.

Abstract. This paper examines the Russo-Ukrainian war across local, regional, and international scales, arguing that the conflict reflects a broader restructuring of the international system. It contends that the Russian invasion was linked to perceptions of declining U.S. hegemony in the aftermath of the pandemic, the European rearmament process following the Merkel era, and growing competition over the shape of a new world order. The study analyzes the strategic interactions among the United States, Europe, Russia, China, and India, proposing that the war in Ukraine has become a key driver of contemporary hegemonic transition and the transformation of twenty-first-century international relations.

**Ledesma, Adrian (2021). Capitalismo pandémico y ¿pospandémico? Herramientas analíticas para su abordaje**
[Pandemic and Post-Pandemic Capitalism? Analytical Tools for Its Interpretation]
Paper presented at the XIV Sociology Conference, Faculty of Social Sciences, University of Buenos Aires (UBA).

XIV Jornadas de Sociología, Facultad de Ciencias Sociales, Universidad de Buenos Aires (UBA), Buenos Aires.

Abstract. This paper examines the concepts of neoliberalism, globalization, mondialization, imperialism, and capitalism in light of the simultaneous pandemic and global economic crises. Through a critical dialogue with diverse theoretical perspectives, it seeks to clarify the analytical boundaries of these categories in order to identify continuities and ruptures within contemporary capitalist social formations. The study also explores the prospects for capitalist transformation in the aftermath of the pandemic and evaluates the challenges posed by these processes for social, environmental, territorial, indigenous, and feminist movements amid the broader political and economic restructuring of the twenty-first century.

### Substack

**Ledesma, Adrian (2025). El Irracionalismo - Introducción**
[Irrationalism: An Introduction]
*Substack — adrianledesma.substack.com* [07-04-2025]

Abstract. This opening piece introduces the theoretical framework for a sustained inquiry into irrationalism as a historical and ideological formation. It argues that irrationalism is not a marginal or deviant tendency but a structurally recurring response to the crises of capitalist modernity, operating at the intersection of mass psychology, political reaction, and the decomposition of scientific materialism. The article establishes the analytical categories — drawn from Lukács, Marx, and historical materialism — that will orient the subsequent series.

◉ ["Full Text (Substack)"](https://adrianledesma.substack.com/p/el-irracionalismo-introduccion)

**Ledesma, Adrian (2026). Los Necromantes del Algoritmo**
[The Algorithm's Necromancers: Influencers, Reward Dynamics, and the Social Base of Political Reaction]
*Substack — adrianledesma.substack.com* [06-06-2026]

Abstract. This article examines how digital influencers, subjected to the reward logic of algorithmic platforms and the commodification of affective bonds, systematically reproduce irrationalism as an ideological form. Drawing on a Marxist framework, it argues that the influencer figure is not merely a cultural phenomenon but a structural node in the consolidation of the social base of political reaction. The piece develops the concept of "algorithmic necromancy" to describe the reanimation of pre-scientific and anti-materialist worldviews through the accelerated circulation of platform content, and situates this dynamic within the broader decomposition of critical consciousness in contemporary Argentina.

◉ ["Full Text (Substack)"](https://adrianledesma.substack.com/p/los-necromantes-del-algoritmo)

**Ledesma, Adrian (2026). Del arte al folklore: el fenómeno ricotero como proceso histórico y cultural (I)**
[From Art to Folklore: The Ricotero Phenomenon as Historical and Cultural Process (I)]
*Substack — adrianledesma.substack.com* [08-06-2026]

Abstract. This article analyzes the mass cultural phenomenon of Los Redonditos de Ricota as a symptom of Argentina's post-pandemic reconstruction of national identity, situated within seven decades of structural economic and political instability. It argues that Argentina is witnessing the emergence of a new popular identity — secular yet quasi-religious — organized around a syncretic pantheon of artists, athletes, saints, and political figures that fuses art, folklore, and collective ritual. The piece draws on Bourdieu's field theory and Gramscian cultural analysis to account for the articulation between aesthetic experience and identity formation in a context of social fragmentation and institutional delegitimation.

◉ ["Full Text (Substack)"](https://adrianledesma.substack.com/p/del-arte-al-folklore-el-fenomeno)

**Ledesma, Adrian (2026). El sujeto-supuesto-matar y violar (I)**
[The Subject-Supposed-to-Kill-and-Rape (I): On the Rupture of the Erotic Bond as Counter-Revolutionary Policy]
*Substack — adrianledesma.substack.com* [02-06-2026]

Abstract. This article examines the political function of the systematic rupture of erotic and social bonds as a state strategy common to both liberal and progressive governments, situated within a shared counter-revolutionary, anti-working-class ontology. Drawing on Lacanian political theory and historical materialism, it argues that the construction of a generalized "subject-supposed-to-kill-and-rape" operates as an ideological mechanism for social individualization, the installation of Hobbesian distrust, and the dissolution of collective solidarity. The piece advances a structural critique of the convergence between reactionary politics and hegemonic feminism in their shared effect of fragmenting working-class subjectivity and social cohesion.

◉ ["Full Text (Substack)"](https://adrianledesma.substack.com/p/el-sujeto-supuesto-matar-y-violar)

**Ledesma, Adrian (2025). La IA, Kubrick y El Viejo Facho - El Instrumento, la Herramienta y el Arma**
[AI, Kubrick, and the Old Fascist: Instrument, Tool, and Weapon]
*Substack — adrianledesma.substack.com* [05-04-2025]

Abstract. This article interrogates the political ontology of artificial intelligence through the analytical triad of instrument, tool, and weapon, using Kubrick's cinema as a critical lens for reading the ideological stakes of technological power. It argues that AI is not a neutral instrument but a materially embedded social relation whose deployment reproduces and intensifies existing structures of domination. The piece situates the figure of "the old fascist" — the reactionary user of technological rationality — as a symptomatic expression of the convergence between technocratic modernization and political authoritarianism in the current conjuncture.

**Ledesma, Adrian (2025). ¿Y si Espert tiene razón? - Mano dura, empleo y socialismo**
[What if Espert Is Right? Hard Line, Employment, and Socialism]
*Substack — adrianledesma.substack.com* [05-02-2025]

Abstract. This article critically engages with the libertarian-right argument, associated with José Luis Espert, that labor market deregulation and punitive social policy constitute effective responses to unemployment and insecurity. Through a historical materialist analysis of the Argentine labor structure, it deconstructs the ideological premises underlying "hard line" policy discourse and exposes its function as a political technology for the subordination of the working class. The piece concludes by articulating the socialist alternative as the only coherent framework for addressing the structural roots of Argentine labor precarity.

◉ ["Full Text (Substack)"](https://adrianledesma.substack.com/p/y-si-espert-tiene-razon)

**Ledesma, Adrian (2025). Arbeit macht frei (unter bestimmten Bedingungen)**
[Work Sets You Free (Under Certain Conditions): Labor, Ideology, and the Limits of Liberal Emancipation]
*Substack — adrianledesma.substack.com* [05-02-2025]

Abstract. Taking its title from the infamous inscription at Auschwitz, this article interrogates the ideological function of labor in liberal and neoliberal discourse, arguing that the promise of emancipation through work operates as a mystification that naturalizes exploitation and forecloses structural critique. It examines the conditions under which labor becomes a mechanism of domination rather than human realization, situating this analysis within the broader Argentine context of austerity, informality, and the dismantling of social protections under the Milei government. The piece proposes a materialist framework for distinguishing emancipatory from disciplinary conceptions of work.

**Ledesma, Adrian (2025). Las cuatro agendas y el pacto ideológico entre liberales - Sexualidad y weimarización de la psicología social argentina**
[The Four Agendas and the Liberal Ideological Pact: Sexuality and the Weimarization of Argentine Social Psychology]
*Substack — adrianledesma.substack.com* [05-02-2025]

Abstract. This article identifies four converging political agendas — libertarian, progressive, identitarian, and securitarian — whose apparent antagonism conceals a shared ideological pact organized around the individualization of social conflict and the suppression of class analysis. It develops the concept of "Weimarization" to characterize the current decomposition of Argentine social psychology: a condition of polarized fragmentation, institutional delegitimation, and mass susceptibility to authoritarian solutions analogous to the crisis of the Weimar Republic. The piece pays particular attention to the role of sexuality and gender politics as terrain of ideological dispute within this configuration.

◉ ["Full Text (Substack)"](https://adrianledesma.substack.com/p/las-cuatro-agendas-y-el-pacto-ideologico)

**Ledesma, Adrian (2025). ¿Hay un auge del fascismo?**
[Is There a Rise of Fascism? Historical Category and Contemporary Political Reaction]
*Substack — adrianledesma.substack.com* [31-01-2025]

Abstract. This article interrogates the analytical validity of "fascism" as a category for understanding contemporary right-wing political formations, including the Milei phenomenon in Argentina and analogous movements globally. Refusing both the inflationary use of the term as a catch-all for authoritarianism and its dismissive rejection as inapplicable to post-Cold War politics, it proposes a historically grounded definition derived from the social and economic conditions of interwar Europe and evaluates the degree to which present conditions reproduce or diverge from that conjuncture. The piece concludes with a materialist assessment of the class character of current reactionary movements.

◉ ["Full Text (Substack)"](https://adrianledesma.substack.com/p/hay-un-auge-del-fascismo-esp-eng)

**Ledesma, Adrian (2025). La vista, el horizonte, la perspectiva histórica y el progreso. I - La vista y la destrucción cognitivo-lingüística**
[Sight, Horizon, Historical Perspective, and Progress. I — Vision and Cognitive-Linguistic Destruction]
*Substack — adrianledesma.substack.com* [29-01-2025]

Abstract. This opening installment of a series on historical perspective examines the systematic destruction of cognitive and linguistic capacities as a political phenomenon — arguing that the erosion of conceptual vocabulary, temporal depth, and analogical reasoning is not a spontaneous cultural tendency but a structurally produced condition of contemporary capitalism. Drawing on Lukács's theory of reification and Benjamin's philosophy of history, it proposes "cognitive-linguistic destruction" as a category for analyzing the degradation of collective capacity for historical thought. The piece situates this process within the Argentine conjuncture of ideological reaction and the broader crisis of critical consciousness.
